Efficiency of heat engines

By 0x7df, Sun 25 September 2016, modified Sun 25 September 2016, in category Misc

$$ \eta = \frac{\mathrm{Useful energy delivered}}{\mathrm{Energy required}} $$
$$ \eta^{\mathrm{HE}} = \frac{\mathrm{Work extracted}}{\mathrm{Heat input}} = \frac{W}{Q_H} $$
$$ \eta^{\mathrm{HE}}_c = \frac{T_H - T_L}{T_H} \le 1 $$

Efficiency of refrigerator

$$ \eta^R = \frac{\mathrm{Heat extracted from }T_L}{\mathrm{Work done}} = \frac{Q}{W} $$
$$ \eta^R_c = \frac{T_L}{T_H - T_L} $$

Efficiency of heat pumps

$$ \eta^{\mathrm{HP}} = \frac{\mathrm{Heat delivered to }T_H}{\mathrm{Work done}} = \frac{Q_H}{W} $$
$$ \eta^{\mathrm{HP}}_c = \frac{T_H}{T_H - T_L} \ge 1 $$
