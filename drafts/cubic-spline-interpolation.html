Cubic spline interpolation

By 0x7df, Wed 03 May 2017, modified Wed 03 May 2017, in category Misc

Given a set of points, \(x_{i-1}, x_i, x_{i+1}, x_{i+2}\), separated by \(\Delta x\), at which some quantity \(y\) has known values \(y_{i-1}, y_i, ...\), respectively, we can find the value \(y_x\) at some arbitrary point \(x\) using cubic spline interpolation.

A cubic polynomial is:

$$ Y_i(z) = a_i z^3 + b_i z^2 + c_i z + d_i $$

where:

$$ z = \frac{x - x_i}{\Delta x} $$

for \(x_i \le x \le x_{i+1}\).

At the left point:

$$ Y_i(0) = d_i = y_i $$

and on the right:

$$ Y_i(1) = a_i + b_i + c_i + d_i = y_{i+1} $$

To provide further conditions we also consider the derivatives:

$$ Y'(z) = 3a_i z^2 + 2b_i z + c_i $$
$$ Y'(0) = c_i $$
$$ Y'(1) = 3a_i + 2b_i + c_i $$

The coefficients are therefore:

$$ a_i = 2(y_i - y_{i+1}) + Y'_i + Y'_{i+1} $$
$$ b_i = 3(y_{i+1} - y_i) - 2Y'_i - Y'_{i+1} $$
$$ c_i = Y'_i $$
$$ d_i = y_i $$

We match up to the second derivatives of the two adjacent piecewise cubic functions at a given point:

$$ Y_{i-1}(1) = Y_i(0) = y_i $$
$$ Y'_{i-1}(1) = Y'_i(0) $$
$$ Y''_{i-1}(1) = Y''_i(0) $$

...

$$ Y_0(0) = y_0 $$
$$ Y_{n-1}(1) = y_n $$

...

$$ Y''_0(0) = 0 $$
$$ Y''_{n-1}(1) = 0 $$
